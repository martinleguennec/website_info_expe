Information about synchronization experiment

First of all, thank you for your interest in my experiment! My name is Martin Le Guennec, I am a PhD student in 2nd year. My research focuses on cross-cultural differences between French and Indian participants when they synchronize with simple rhythms, and this is why I need your help.

All you will have to do is to tap your finger on a table along with rhythmic sounds. You will perform different tasks several times, each lasting between 1 and 2 minutes. This is fun and really simple, it will only take 1 hour of your time and it would help me tremendously with my research.

The experiment takes place at the EuroMov Digital Health in Motion laboratory (Université de Montpellier) (700 Av. du Pic Saint-Loup, 34090 Montpellier). You can access with public transport:

  • Tramway 1 (Occitanie)
  • Bus 6 (Ambroise Paré)
  • Bus 23/26 (Pic Saint-Loup)
  • Navette A/B (Pic Saint-Loup)
